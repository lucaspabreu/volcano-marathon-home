About the Race

Run between
the Moai.

The Volcano Marathon® is a full 42.195 km race held on Easter Island — a remote volcanic outpost 3,700 km off the coast of Chile. Runners cross extinct craters, lava fields and Polynesian archaeological sites, finishing beneath the gaze of the iconic Moai statues.

It is part marathon, part pilgrimage — a five-day expedition that combines a world-class road race with cultural immersion in Rapa Nui, one of the most enigmatic places on the planet.

Five days on the island.

A complete 5-day expedition — fly into Mataveri Airport (IPC), settle into Hanga Roa, spend the days exploring Moai, craters and coastline, race on Sunday and recover with the team before flying out.

Train hard. Pack light.

The Volcano Marathon is a road marathon on volcanic terrain — no extreme cold, no special gear. What you need is a solid 16-week build, a pair of broken-in road shoes, and the discipline to taper.
