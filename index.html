Heritage · 02

12 editions.
one original event.

The World Marathon Challenge® has produced 7 marathons on 7 continents in 7 days — every January, every year, since 2015. Every record on this timeline was set on the official course, under the official 168-hour clock.

2015
Inaugural
First edition. David Gething & Marianna Zaikova champions.
2016
First US Woman
Becca Pizzi becomes first US woman to complete 7·7·7.
2017
Men's Record
Michael Wardian sets fastest average — 2:45:57 per marathon.
2019
Women's Record
Susannah Gill: 3:28:09 women's average-time record.
2020
First Woman Overall
Kristina Schou Madsen wins outright — 3:25:57 average.
2025
Records Fall
Estefanía Únzu Ripoll & Tomomi Bitoh set new women's marks.
2026
11th Edition
Wardian & Reed champions. Oldest woman finisher: age 70.

Organized by RunBuk · 06

Delivered by a team,
not a name.

The World Marathon Challenge® is operated by RunBuk — a specialist team that designs, builds and runs world-class endurance events on every continent.

01
Race Management
Race direction, course certification, timing, results, and athlete operations across all seven legs.
02
Global Logistics
Charter aviation, ground transport, customs, and venue coordination across seven continents in 168 hours.
03
Medical & Performance
On-site physicians, physiotherapists, nutrition, hydration, and recovery protocols between every marathon.
04
Media & Broadcast
In-house photo, video, and press operations. Handles accreditation, b-roll, embeds, and live story feeds.
05
Athlete Services
Briefings, gear, accommodation, and round-the-clock support for every competitor on the field.
